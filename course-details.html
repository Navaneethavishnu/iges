IGES collaborated with BSW during Speranza to screen a film titled “Phools for love” made by an organisation, Agents of Ishq. The objective of the session was to break the ice so that the students could talk about the emotion ‘Love’ and their diverse understanding of it, to celebrate diversity with a loving heart and pause for a moment to reflect on one’s own ideas of love and emotions.
As part of the short discussion series, IGES undertook an interactive session on the recent Judgement on Section 377, in September 2018. A panel of experts and students who have been involved with the petition on Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code, engaged in discussion with over 15 students.
IGES held a student interaction kiosk at the July 2018 orientation for the new UG, PG and PhD students. More than 950 UG students visited the kiosk and interacted with the IGES members to understand its mandate. IGES developed a poster on the initiative of supernumerary seats and made a short video to play at the kiosk. 1100 PG students attended a separate session organised by IGES to make them aware of its objectives and mandate.
IIT's JAB Committee launched an initiative to boost the admission of young women in STEM subjects at IITs in India. The supernumerary scheme is an effort to increase seats to include more JEE Adv. qualified women candidates. IGES counselled 175 women students & their parents in June 2018 to help understand IIT opportunities and careers for women in STEM. The scheme of supernumerary seats, coupled with our intensive outreach efforts, appears to have been an unqualified success. This year 143 girls have been offered undergraduate seats at IIT Delhi, which is 15.7% of the total offers made. Both in absolute and percentage terms, the numbers have nearly doubled in the last 2 years!
